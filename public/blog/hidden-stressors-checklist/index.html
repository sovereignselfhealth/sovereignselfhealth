3 Hidden Stressors Sabotaging Your Energy

Most people address sleep and diet, but miss these three sneaky sources of chronic stress that drain energy.

You’ve tried everything: more sleep, cleaner diet, supplements. But you still wake up exhausted.

The problem? You’re addressing the obvious stressors while missing the hidden ones.

1. Blue Light After Dark

Your circadian rhythm depends on darkness signals. Even dim screens tell your brain it’s still daytime, suppressing melatonin for hours.

What to do: Install f.lux on your computer, use blue-blocking glasses after 8 PM, or switch devices to grayscale mode.

2. Mouth Breathing During Sleep

Breathing through your mouth all night triggers stress hormones, disrupts CO2 levels, and prevents deep sleep.

What to do: Try mouth tape (3M Micropore tape). Sounds weird, works brilliantly.

3. Chronic Under-Eating

“Eating less” seems healthy, but if you’re under-eating protein or carbs chronically, your body interprets it as famine—a massive stressor.

What to do: Track your intake for 3 days. Most people discover they’re eating 30-40% less than they thought.
