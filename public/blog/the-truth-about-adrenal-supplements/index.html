The Truth About Adrenal Supplements (What Works, What Doesn't)

Spending $200/month on adrenal supplements but still exhausted? Here's what actually works for HPA axis dysfunction—and what's just expensive pee.

You’re exhausted all the time. You can’t wake up in the morning. You crash every afternoon. You’re wired at night despite being bone-tired.

Someone tells you about “adrenal support” supplements. You start Googling.

Suddenly, you’re overwhelmed:

  • Ashwagandha
  • Rhodiola
  • Holy basil
  • Licorice root
  • Adrenal cortex extract
  • Adrenal glandulars
  • Vitamin C
  • B vitamins
  • Phosphatidylserine

You buy a “complete adrenal support formula” for $60 a bottle. You take it religiously for a month.

And… nothing changes.

Maybe you feel slightly better for a week, then it wears off. Or maybe you feel worse—more anxious, more wired, sleeping even less.

So you buy a different brand. Then another. You’re spending $150-200/month on adrenal supplements, and you’re still exhausted.

Here’s the problem: You’re guessing.

Not all adrenal supplements work for all cortisol patterns. Some help high cortisol. Some help low cortisol. Some do both (adaptogens). And some are complete BS.

If you take the wrong supplement for YOUR cortisol pattern, you’ll either get no results or feel worse.

Let me break down what actually works, what doesn’t, and how to stop wasting money on supplements that aren’t right for YOUR body.

First: “Adrenal Support” Isn’t One Thing

The term “adrenal support” is vague and misleading.

What are you actually trying to support?

  1. Low morning cortisol? (Can’t wake up, need caffeine to function)
  2. High evening cortisol? (Wired at night, can’t fall asleep)
  3. Elevated cortisol all day? (Anxious, jittery, can’t relax)
  4. Crashed cortisol all day? (Exhausted 24/7, no energy reserves)
  5. Inverted rhythm? (Low in morning, high at night—the worst combo)

Each of these patterns requires a DIFFERENT approach.

Taking a generic “adrenal support” formula is like taking “heart medication” without knowing if you have high blood pressure, low blood pressure, or arrhythmia.

You need to know YOUR cortisol pattern before you can support it effectively.

The Supplements That Actually Work (When Used Correctly)

Let’s break down the most common adrenal supplements—what they do, when to use them, and when NOT to use them.

1. Adaptogenic Herbs (The “Smart” Supplements)

Adaptogens are herbs that help your body ADAPT to stress. They’re “bidirectional”—meaning they can raise or lower cortisol depending on what your body needs.

The main adaptogens:

Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera)

Best for: High cortisol (especially elevated evening cortisol)

What it does:

  • Lowers cortisol (particularly at night)
  • Reduces anxiety and stress perception
  • Improves sleep quality
  • Supports thyroid function (increases T3 and T4 production)

Dosage: 300-600mg standardized extract (at least 5% withanolides), taken in the evening

When NOT to use:

  • If your cortisol is already LOW (it can lower it further)
  • If you have hyperthyroidism (it stimulates thyroid production)
  • During pregnancy

My take: Ashwagandha is one of the best for people who are “wired but tired”—elevated evening cortisol preventing sleep. If you can’t shut your brain off at night, this is your friend.

Rhodiola (Rhodiola rosea)

Best for: Low cortisol (especially low morning cortisol and fatigue)

What it does:

  • Supports energy and stamina
  • Improves mental clarity and focus
  • Helps the body respond to stress without spiking cortisol excessively
  • Increases serotonin and dopamine (mood support)

Dosage: 200-400mg standardized extract (3% rosavins, 1% salidroside), taken in the morning or early afternoon

When NOT to use:

  • If your cortisol is already HIGH (it can be stimulating)
  • Late in the day (it can interfere with sleep)
  • If you have bipolar disorder (can trigger manic episodes)

My take: Rhodiola is great for people who wake up exhausted and can’t get going. It’s like a natural, sustainable energy boost—not a stimulant, but it helps your body produce energy more efficiently.

Holy Basil / Tulsi (Ocimum sanctum)

Best for: Balanced cortisol support, anxiety, blood sugar regulation

What it does:

  • Modulates cortisol (not too high, not too low)
  • Reduces anxiety and promotes calm focus
  • Supports blood sugar balance (important because blood sugar crashes trigger cortisol spikes)
  • Anti-inflammatory

Dosage: 300-600mg standardized extract, can be taken morning or evening

When NOT to use:

  • If trying to conceive or pregnant (traditionally used as a contraceptive in Ayurveda)

My take: Holy basil is the “gentle” adaptogen. Good for people who are stressed but don’t have extreme high or low cortisol. Also great if blood sugar dysregulation is part of your picture.

Eleuthero / Siberian Ginseng (Eleutherococcus senticosus)

Best for: Low cortisol, chronic fatigue, physical stamina

What it does:

  • Increases energy and endurance
  • Supports immune function
  • Helps the body handle physical and mental stress
  • Improves focus

Dosage: 300-1200mg daily, taken in the morning

When NOT to use:

  • If cortisol is already high (can be overstimulating)
  • If you have high blood pressure

My take: Eleuthero is for people who are physically and mentally depleted. Good for athletes or people recovering from chronic illness.

2. Phosphatidylserine (For High Evening Cortisol)

Best for: Elevated evening cortisol (can’t fall asleep, wired at night)

What it does:

  • Blunts cortisol response (especially evening cortisol)
  • Improves sleep onset and quality
  • Supports cognitive function and memory

Dosage: 300-400mg before bed

When NOT to use:

  • If your cortisol is LOW (it will lower it further)
  • During the day (you need cortisol during the day for energy)

My take: This is one of my go-to recommendations for people with inverted cortisol curves (low in the morning, high at night). Phosphatidylserine at night helps bring evening cortisol down so you can actually fall asleep.

3. Licorice Root (For LOW Morning Cortisol)

Best for: Low morning cortisol, low blood pressure, salt cravings

What it does:

  • Slows the breakdown of cortisol (so existing cortisol stays in circulation longer)
  • Raises blood pressure slightly (good if you have low BP and feel dizzy when standing)
  • Anti-inflammatory

Dosage: 300-500mg in the morning (use DGL—deglycyrrhizinated licorice—if you have high blood pressure)

When NOT to use:

  • If you have HIGH cortisol (it will make it worse)
  • If you have high blood pressure (it raises BP)
  • Long-term (use for 4-8 weeks max, then take a break)

My take: Licorice root is powerful for low morning cortisol—but it’s not something you stay on forever. Use it short-term while you address the ROOT causes of low cortisol (poor sleep, blood sugar dysregulation, chronic stress, gut infections).

4. Vitamin C (Universal Adrenal Support)

Best for: Everyone (especially if you’re under chronic stress)

What it does:

  • Your adrenal glands have the highest concentration of Vitamin C in the body
  • Vitamin C is required for cortisol production
  • Under stress, your adrenals deplete Vitamin C rapidly
  • Supports immune function (stress weakens immunity)

Dosage: 1000-2000mg daily, split into 2-3 doses

When NOT to use:

  • No contraindications (everyone benefits from Vitamin C)

My take: Vitamin C is foundational. If you’re stressed and your adrenals are working overtime, you’re burning through Vitamin C. Replenish it.

5. B Vitamins (Energy Production and Stress Response)

Best for: Everyone under chronic stress, fatigue, brain fog

What they do:

  • B5 (pantothenic acid) is specifically required for cortisol production
  • B6 supports neurotransmitter production (serotonin, dopamine, GABA)
  • B12 and folate support energy production (Krebs cycle)
  • Stress depletes B vitamins rapidly

Dosage: B-complex (contains all B vitamins in balanced ratios), taken in the morning

When NOT to use:

  • No contraindications (everyone needs B vitamins)

My take: B vitamins are foundational. If you’re stressed, fatigued, or have poor energy production (which you can see on an Organic Acids Test), B vitamins are non-negotiable.

6. Magnesium (Stress, Sleep, and Muscle Relaxation)

Best for: Everyone (most people are deficient)

What it does:

  • Calms the nervous system (supports parasympathetic activation)
  • Improves sleep quality
  • Reduces muscle tension and cramping
  • Supports energy production (required for ATP synthesis)
  • Regulates cortisol

Dosage: 400-600mg before bed (use magnesium glycinate or magnesium threonate for best absorption and sleep support)

When NOT to use:

  • If you have kidney disease (consult your doctor first)

My take: Magnesium is one of the most underrated supplements. It’s critical for stress management, sleep, and energy. Almost everyone benefits from it.

7. Adrenal Glandulars and Adrenal Cortex Extracts (Use with Caution)

What they are: Dried and powdered adrenal glands from animals (usually cows)

The claim: Provides “raw materials” for your adrenals to rebuild and function better

The reality: Mixed evidence, and potentially risky

Potential benefits:

  • May provide nutrients, cofactors, and hormones that support adrenal function
  • Some people report feeling more energy

Potential risks:

  • May contain actual cortisol or cortisol precursors (can suppress your body’s own production)
  • Quality and purity vary wildly (risk of contamination, heavy metals, prions)
  • Can overstimulate (especially if you already have high cortisol)

My take: I generally avoid glandulars. The risk-to-benefit ratio isn’t worth it when there are safer, more effective options (adaptogens, nutrients, lifestyle). If you do use them, work with a practitioner and use high-quality sources only.

The Supplements That DON’T Work (Or Are Overrated)

1. Generic “Adrenal Support” Formulas (The Kitchen Sink Approach)

The problem:

  • They throw 15 ingredients into one formula
  • Low doses of each ingredient (underdosed = ineffective)
  • No consideration for YOUR specific cortisol pattern
  • Expensive and ineffective

Example: A formula with ashwagandha + rhodiola + licorice + holy basil + B vitamins + Vitamin C + glandulars.

Sounds great, right? Covers all the bases?

Wrong.

  • If your cortisol is HIGH, the rhodiola and licorice will make it worse
  • If your cortisol is LOW, the ashwagandha will make it worse
  • The doses are so low they won’t do anything anyway

You’re better off taking 2-3 targeted supplements at effective doses than a kitchen-sink formula.

2. DHEA Supplements (Without Testing)

What it is: DHEA is a hormone produced by the adrenals (a precursor to testosterone and estrogen)

The claim: Low DHEA = adrenal fatigue, so supplement with DHEA

The problem:

  • DHEA is a HORMONE, not a vitamin
  • Taking it without knowing your levels is dangerous
  • Can convert to estrogen or testosterone (can worsen hormonal imbalances)
  • Can cause acne, hair loss, mood changes, aggression

My take: NEVER take DHEA without testing your levels first. If you’re low, work with a practitioner to dose appropriately. If you’re NOT low, you’re just creating hormonal chaos.

3. Cortisol Supplements (Yes, People Sell These)

What they are: Actual cortisol (hydrocortisone) or cortisol analogs

The problem:

  • Cortisol is a prescription medication (hydrocortisone)
  • Taking exogenous cortisol suppresses your body’s own production
  • Creates dependency
  • Can lead to Cushing’s syndrome (too much cortisol)

My take: Stay away. This is not something to mess with unless you have diagnosed Addison’s disease and are under medical supervision.

What to Do Instead: Test First, Supplement Specifically

Okay, so you can’t just throw random adrenal supplements at the problem. What’s the right approach?

Step 1: Test Your Cortisol Rhythm

You MUST know your cortisol pattern before supplementing.

Run a 4-point cortisol curve (saliva or urine):

  • Measures cortisol at 4 times throughout the day
  • Shows your actual rhythm (not just one morning value)
  • Identifies if you’re high, low, or dysregulated

Also test DHEA (your stress resilience hormone)

Example findings:

Time Your Cortisol Optimal Range Supplement Strategy
Morning (8am) 3.2 ng/mL 13-24 ng/mL LOW → Rhodiola, Licorice (short-term), Vitamin C, B vitamins
Noon 5.1 ng/mL 5-10 ng/mL Borderline low → Holy basil for balance
4pm 4.8 ng/mL 3-8 ng/mL Normal → No intervention needed
Night (10pm) 8.7 ng/mL <4 ng/mL HIGH → Ashwagandha, Phosphatidylserine, Magnesium

Now you have a personalized supplement plan based on YOUR data, not guesswork.

Step 2: Start with Lifestyle (Supplements Alone Won’t Fix This)

Supplements support—they don’t replace lifestyle changes.

If you’re not doing these, no supplement will work:

Sleep:

  • 7-9 hours nightly
  • Consistent bedtime and wake time
  • Dark, cool room
  • Morning sunlight exposure (supports cortisol awakening response)

Blood Sugar Balance:

  • Eat protein at every meal (30g minimum)
  • Don’t skip breakfast
  • Eat every 3-4 hours (prevent blood sugar crashes that spike cortisol)

Stress Management:

  • Breathwork, meditation, yoga
  • Boundaries (stop overcommitting)
  • Time in nature

Exercise (Appropriately):

  • If cortisol is HIGH: Avoid intense workouts (they spike cortisol further)
  • If cortisol is LOW: Avoid overtraining (it depletes you further)
  • Focus on walking, gentle yoga, light strength training

Step 3: Supplement Strategically Based on Your Pattern

For LOW Morning Cortisol:

  • Rhodiola (200-400mg in the morning)
  • Licorice root (300-500mg in the morning, short-term)
  • Vitamin C (1000-2000mg daily)
  • B-complex (morning)

For HIGH Evening Cortisol:

  • Ashwagandha (300-600mg in the evening)
  • Phosphatidylserine (300-400mg before bed)
  • Magnesium glycinate (400-600mg before bed)

For Balanced Support (Mild Dysregulation):

  • Holy basil (300-600mg daily)
  • Vitamin C (1000-2000mg daily)
  • B-complex (morning)
  • Magnesium (400-600mg before bed)

Step 4: Address Root Causes (Why Is Your HPA Axis Dysregulated?)

Supplements and lifestyle help—but if you don’t address WHY your cortisol is dysregulated, you’ll stay stuck.

Common root causes:

1. Gut Dysfunction

  • Infections (bacteria, parasites, yeast)
  • Dysbiosis (imbalanced gut bacteria)
  • Intestinal permeability (leaky gut)
  • Chronic inflammation triggers cortisol production

Test: GI-MAP stool test

2. Blood Sugar Dysregulation

  • Every blood sugar crash triggers a cortisol spike
  • Chronic blood sugar swings = chronic cortisol dysregulation

Test: Fasting glucose, fasting insulin, HbA1c, consider a continuous glucose monitor

3. Chronic Inflammation

  • Food sensitivities
  • Hidden infections
  • Autoimmune conditions
  • Obesity (fat cells produce inflammatory cytokines)

Test: Food sensitivity panel (IgG), CRP, ESR, autoimmune markers

4. Thyroid Dysfunction

  • Hypothyroidism and hyperthyroidism both affect cortisol
  • Low thyroid → body compensates with high cortisol
  • High thyroid → anxiety, elevated cortisol

Test: Full thyroid panel (TSH, fT3, fT4, rT3, TPO, Tg antibodies)

5. Nutrient Deficiencies

  • B vitamins, Vitamin C, magnesium, Vitamin D
  • Your adrenals can’t function without these

Test: Organic Acids Test (OAT) or Metabolic Wellness Profile

Step 5: Retest at 90 Days

Supplements and lifestyle changes take time to work.

After 90 days:

  • Retest your 4-point cortisol curve
  • See if your rhythm has normalized
  • Adjust supplements based on progress

Most people see significant improvement in 3-6 months if they’re addressing root causes + using the right supplements for THEIR pattern.

The Bottom Line: Stop Guessing, Start Testing

Here’s what you need to know:

Adrenal supplements CAN work—but only if:

  1. You’re taking the RIGHT supplements for YOUR cortisol pattern
  2. You’re using effective doses (not underdosed formulas)
  3. You’re addressing lifestyle and root causes simultaneously

Adrenal supplements WON’T work if:

  1. You’re guessing (taking whatever worked for your friend)
  2. You’re using generic “adrenal support” formulas
  3. You’re ignoring sleep, stress, blood sugar, and gut health

The solution:

  • Test your cortisol rhythm (4-point curve)
  • Identify YOUR pattern (high, low, dysregulated)
  • Supplement specifically for YOUR needs
  • Address root causes (gut, blood sugar, inflammation, thyroid)
  • Retest and adjust

Stop wasting money on supplements that aren’t right for YOUR body.

Start testing, start targeting, start seeing results.
